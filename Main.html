Module 1: What is AI?

This module introduces the basics of Artificial Intelligence (AI), Generative AI, and Large Language Models (LLMs). You'll learn how these technologies create new things and how they "learn" from information.

What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)?

AI refers to computer systems that can do tasks that usually need human intelligence. This includes learning, solving problems, making decisions, and understanding language. Think of it as a smart tool for your Elektro work!

What is Generative AI?

Generative AI is a special type of AI that createsTo make or produce something new. new and original contentInformation, ideas, or artistic works.. It doesn't just copy; it makes new text, images, audio, or video based on what it has learned. It's like it's predicting what should come next in a pattern.

Examples of Generative AI tools:

📝 Text Generation (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini)

Writes emails, stories, code, answers questions.

🖼️ Image Generation (e.g., DALL-E, Midjourney)

Creates pictures from text descriptions.

🎬 Video Generation (e.g., Synthesia)

Creates videos from text.

What are Large Language Models (LLMs)?

LLMs are AI that understand and generate human-like text. Think of phone autocomplete, but much more advanced! They are "large" because they learn from huge amounts of text data.

Analogy: Imagine a student who has read almost every book and website. They become very good at knowing how words fit together. LLMs are like that, but they are computer programs.

Training Data: What LLMs "Read"

LLMs learn by being "trained" on massive amounts of text and code. This training dataInformation used to teach an AI system. can include:

  • Books
  • Websites (Wikipedia, news, blogs)
  • Research papers
  • Code

The AI reads billions of text samples to learn patternsA regularly repeated arrangement or sequence., context, and grammar.

Illustrative: Types of data LLMs learn from.

The Core Task: Predicting the Next Word

LLMs work by predictingTo say what you think will happen in the future. what word is most likely to come next in a sentence. For example, if you see "The cat sat on the ____," you might say "mat." An LLM does something similar based on patterns it learned.

Module 2: AI's Mistakes & Dangers

AI is powerful, but it's not perfect. This module covers AI "hallucinations" (mistakes), misinformation, bias, and other risks like how AI can affect our thinking and privacy. Understanding these dangers helps us use AI wisely.

What are AI HallucinationsIn AI, when the model generates incorrect or nonsensical information but presents it as fact.?

An AI hallucination is when an LLM generates information that is factually incorrect, nonsensical, or unrelated to your question, but it sounds confident. The AI "makes things up."

Examples:

  • Saying a telescope discovered something it didn't.
  • Giving a recipe with glue as an ingredient.
  • Image AIs making people with six fingers.

Why do LLMs hallucinate?

  • They predict words based on probability; sometimes they pick a wrong but pattern-fitting word.
  • Their training data might have errors, biases, or be outdated.
  • They don't "understand" truth like humans do; they just match patterns.
  • They are often programmed to give an answer, even if they don't "know."

AI and MisinformationFalse or inaccurate information, especially that which is deliberately intended to deceive.

Generative AI can quickly create convincing but false text, images, or videos. This is called misinformation.

DeepfakesAI-generated videos or audio recordings that look and sound like real people saying or doing things they never did.: These are AI-made videos/audio that mimic real people. They can be used to spread false news or damage reputations. For example, a fake video of a politician.

Remember: Always check information from trusted sources, especially if it seems surprising or shocking!

AI BiasWhen an AI system shows prejudice for or against certain things, people, or groups, often learned from biased training data.: When AI is Unfair

AI bias happens when an AI system gives prejudiced results. This is often because its training data had societal biases (e.g., about race, gender, age).

Examples of AI Bias:

  • An AI image generator mostly shows men when asked for "CEO pictures."
  • A hiring AI unfairly filters out good candidates from certain groups if trained on biased data.
  • Facial recognition working poorly for some ethnic groups if not trained on diverse faces.

Impact: Bias can lead to unfair outcomes, like denying jobs or services to certain people.

Cognitive Risks: Is AI Making Us Lazy Thinkers?

Cognitive riskThe danger of weakening our thinking skills by relying too much on AI. means AI might hurt our thinking skills.

  • Cognitive Offloading: Letting AI do the thinking for us. If we always use AI, we might not develop our own problem-solving skills.
  • Reduced Critical Thinking: If we use AI for answers without doing the research ourselves, we miss learning to analyze and judge information.
  • Over-reliance: Depending too much on AI. It's important to struggle and learn by figuring things out ourselves.

Analogy: Your brain is like a muscle. If AI always does the "heavy lifting" (thinking), your brain "muscle" might not get strong.

Ethical Concerns: Plagiarism, Privacy, and Jobs

  • PlagiarismCopying someone else's work or ideas and pretending they are your own.: Using AI to write your assignment and saying it's your own work is plagiarism. This is serious. Always do your own thinking first.
  • Data PrivacyKeeping personal information safe and not sharing it without permission.: AI tools might collect your data (questions, text). Be careful about sharing sensitive personal or company information, especially with free AI tools.
  • Impact on Jobs: AI might change jobs by automating some tasks. This means learning new skills and adapting will be important for electricians too.

Module 3: AI as Your Smart Assistant

While there are dangers, AI can also be a very helpful tool, especially for learning and work. This module explores how you can use AI for brainstorming ideas and, most importantly, for refining and improving work you've already done.

AI for BrainstormingGenerating many ideas quickly, often in a group. and Idea Generation

If you're stuck, AI can help you get initial ideas. You guide the AI and then critically evaluate its suggestions.

Example for Elektro: You could ask an AI: "Give me 5 ideas for a simple electrical circuit project for beginners."

Important: AI's ideas are just a starting point. You must use your own knowledge to choose and develop them. It's about sparking your creativity, not replacing it.

  • Ensure components are not powered.

The Best Use: AI for RefiningTo improve something by making small changes. Your Own Work

A powerful and ethical way to use AI is to improve work you have already created. After you write a draft or solve a problem, AI can be a helpful reviewer.

AI can help with:

  • Grammar and Spelling
  • Style and Tone (making writing clearer or more formal)
  • Clarity (fixing confusing sentences)
  • Word Choice (suggesting better words)
  • Summarizing your own text
  • Getting feedback (e.g., "Is this explanation clear?")

Crucial: You do the main thinking and creating. AI is then a tool for editing and feedback. This helps you learn!

Module 4: AI in Your (Elektro) World

AI is not just a general technology; it's starting to impact specific fields, including electrical work. This module looks at how AI might affect your future career, reminds you about data privacy, and helps you think about class rules for using AI.

AI in the Elektro World and Technical Jobs

AI is changing industries, including skilled trades. Here's how it might impact Elektro:

  • More Jobs for AI Infrastructure: AI needs data centers, which need electricians!
  • AI as a Tool for Electricians:
    • Monitoring electrical systems remotely.
    • Diagnosing complex faults.
    • Predicting when equipment might fail (predictive maintenance).
    • Helping design electrical layouts.
    • Quick access to technical guides via AI chatbots.
  • Changes in Job Tasks: Some routine tasks might be AI-assisted. Electricians might focus more on complex problem-solving and overseeing AI systems.
  • Need for New Skills: "AI literacy" – understanding how to work with AI tools and interpret their outputs – will be important. Adaptability and lifelong learning are key!

Data Privacy Reminder

When using AI tools, especially for work:

  • Avoid inputting sensitive client information or confidential company data into public AI models.
  • Be aware that your interactions might be logged or used for AI training.
  • Norway has a National Strategy for AI that includes protecting privacy.

Course Recap & Final Thoughts

We've covered a lot! Remember these key points:

  • AI (especially Generative AI and LLMs) can create new content by predicting patterns.
  • AI can make mistakes (hallucinations) and has dangers like misinformation and bias.
  • Using AI too much can weaken your thinking skills (cognitive risk).
  • Always be ethical: don't plagiarize and protect privacy.
  • AI is a powerful tool for brainstorming and, most importantly, for refining YOUR OWN work.
  • For Elektro students, AI will be a tool that changes how work is done. Be ready to learn and adapt!

The most important message: AI is a powerful tool, but it requires your critical thinking and responsible use.
